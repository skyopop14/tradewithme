01 — Overview

WHAT IS THIS STRATEGY?

This strategy combines the Engulfing candlestick pattern with the RSI (14-period) to confirm momentum before entering options. The engulfing candle gives the direction. RSI confirms the strength. Both must align. Use the tabs above to go deep into each setup.

01 — Pattern Definition

WHAT IS A SHOOTING STAR?

A Shooting Star is a single-candle bearish reversal pattern that appears after an uptrend or bullish push. It has a small real body at the bottom, a very long upper wick (at least 2× the body), and little to no lower wick.

What it tells you: price opened, rallied aggressively (buyers pushed it high), but then sellers took complete control and drove price back down near the open — rejecting the highs. The long upper wick is the "failed attempt" by buyers. It is your signal to buy a Put.

  • Small real body at the bottom of the candle (can be red or green — red is stronger)
  • Long upper wick = at least 2× the length of the real body
  • Little or no lower wick — sellers dominated the close
  • Appears after an uptrend or bullish push near resistance

WHAT TO AVOID
  • Upper wick less than 2× the body — pattern is weak
  • Long lower wick present — buying pressure came back
  • Pattern forms in middle of a range (no resistance above)
  • No prior uptrend — reversal pattern needs something to reverse
  • Very low volume on the candle — no institutional selling

💡 Shooting Star vs Inverted Hammer: Both have the same shape — small body, long upper wick. The difference is context. A Shooting Star appears after an uptrend = bearish signal (Buy Put). An Inverted Hammer appears after a downtrend = bullish signal (Buy Call). Always check the prior trend before acting.

03 — Execution

STEP-BY-STEP: SHOOTING STAR PUT ENTRY

  1. 1
    Identify the Uptrend First — Shooting Star only works after an uptrend or bullish rally. If Nifty has been falling, ignore this pattern. The reversal needs something to reverse from.
  2. 2
    Mark Resistance Pre-Market — Before 9:15 AM, mark previous day high, VWAP, all-time highs, and round numbers. The Shooting Star must form at or very near one of these levels for a valid signal.
  3. 3
    Wait for the Candle to FULLY CLOSE — Never enter mid-candle. The long upper wick must be fully formed. A candle with a long upper wick mid-way could still recover and close bullish. Always wait.
  4. 4
    Verify the Anatomy — Upper wick must be at least 2× the real body. Almost no lower wick. Small body. If the lower wick is significant (more than 30% of upper wick), reduce position size or skip.
  5. 5
    Check RSI at Candle Close — RSI must be above 60 and turning down. If RSI is below 55, skip this trade regardless of how perfect the candle looks. Both must confirm.
  6. 6
    Check Volume — Shooting Star should have above-average volume. High volume on the rejection wick means institutional sellers aggressively pushed price down from the highs. Low volume = weak signal.
  7. 7
    Wait for Next Candle Confirmation (Optional but Powerful) — For extra safety, wait for the next candle to open and start moving lower. If it opens bearish (below the Shooting Star close), your entry is confirmed. Enter ATM Put at this point.
  8. 8
    Set Stop Loss — SL = above the HIGH of the Shooting Star candle (the tip of the upper wick). If price closes above this level, the rejection has failed — exit immediately without hesitation.
  9. 9
    Book Target / Trail SL — Target 1.5×–2× SL distance (5-min) or 2×–3× (15-min). Trail SL to the high of each bearish candle. Exit when a bullish candle with a long lower wick (Hammer) appears.
04 — Pattern Comparison

SHOOTING STAR vs BEARISH ENGULFING

Both are bearish reversal patterns that signal Put entries — but they have different strengths. Knowing when to use which gives you an edge.

🌠
SHOOTING STAR
  • Single candle — signal forms and completes in one bar
  • Best at extreme highs — all-time highs, monthly resistance
  • RSI needs to be above 60–70 to confirm overextension
  • Appears more often on 15-min charts with cleaner signals
  • SL is placed above the wick high — often a wider stop
  • Consider waiting for next candle confirmation for safety
↓
BEARISH ENGULFING
  • Two candles — requires prior green candle to engulf
  • Best at regular resistance — VWAP, swing highs, S/R zones
  • RSI needs to be above 50 (lower bar)
  • Appears regularly on both 5-min and 15-min
  • SL placed above candle high — tighter stop possible
  • Entry at open of next candle after confirmation

🌠 Power Combo: When a Shooting Star AND a Bearish Engulfing appear at the same resistance level (within 1–2 candles of each other), that is an extremely powerful confluence. The market is clearly rejecting that price zone from two different angles. This is a high-confidence put entry setup.

01 — Foundation

WHY S&R IS THE MOST RELIABLE STRATEGY

Support and Resistance is not just a strategy — it is the language of the market. Every institutional order, every algo, every professional trader's decision revolves around key price levels. When you trade at these levels with a confirming candlestick pattern and RSI backing, you are trading in alignment with the biggest players in the market.

This is why it has the highest win rate among all intraday strategies when applied correctly — you are not guessing direction, you are waiting for price to come to a proven zone and react.

S
Support Level

A price zone where buying pressure has historically overwhelmed selling. Price bounces from here. Buy Calls when price revisits support.

R
Resistance Level

A price zone where selling pressure has historically overwhelmed buying. Price rejects from here. Buy Puts when price revisits resistance.

C
Candlestick Pattern

The trigger. A reversal candle at S/R tells you the level is being respected right now. Without a candle signal, S/R alone is not enough.

V
Volume + RSI

Confirms the reaction is real. High volume at S/R + RSI in the right zone = institutional players are respecting that level.

02 — Drawing Levels

HOW TO IDENTIFY KEY S&R LEVELS (PRE-MARKET)

The most important skill in this strategy is knowing which levels matter. Draw these every day before 9:15 AM on your Nifty / Bank Nifty chart:

📅 Previous Day High & Low

The single most important levels for intraday. PDH = resistance. PDL = support. Price respects these almost every single day on Nifty.

🔵 VWAP (Dynamic S/R)

Resets every day at 9:15 AM. Price above VWAP = buyers in control. Below VWAP = sellers in control. Also acts as real-time support/resistance throughout the day.

🔢 Round Numbers

22,000 / 22,500 / 23,000 etc. Psychological levels where large option strikes are placed. Price reacts very sharply at these levels on Nifty.

📊 Weekly High & Low

On Thursdays (expiry day) these become critical. Mark the current week's high and low — breakouts of these levels cause explosive moves.

🕯️ Previous Day Close

A subtle but important level. Price often tests yesterday's close early in the session. If it holds, it acts as support; if broken, acts as resistance.

📈 Swing Highs & Lows

On the 15-min chart, mark the last 2–3 swing highs and lows. These are the most "natural" support and resistance levels respected by price action.

💡 Rule of Thumb: The more times a level has been tested and held, the stronger it is. A level that has been tested 3+ times without breaking is a major S/R zone — the most powerful zones for your trade entries.

03 — Call Setup (At Support)

BUY CALL — PRICE AT SUPPORT

When price arrives at a strong support level, you are looking for a bullish reversal candle pattern to confirm the bounce. Here are the 3 best candle signals at support:

Bullish Engulfing

Green body fully covers prior red body at support. The strongest reversal signal at a support zone. Combined with RSI below 50 turning up.

Hammer

Small body at top, long lower wick (2× body). Sellers tried to push lower but buyers rejected the move. Very common at support in Nifty.

Bullish Pin Bar

Long lower wick that pierces below support but closes back above it. The "rejection wick" is the most honest signal that support is strong.

↑
CALL ENTRY — FULL CHECKLIST
  • Price is at a pre-marked support level (PDL, round number, VWAP, swing low)
  • Bullish reversal candle closes at or touching the support zone (Engulfing / Hammer / Pin Bar)
  • RSI is below 50 and turning up — or ideally in the 35–50 zone rising. Divergence is strongest signal.
  • Volume spike on the reversal candle — confirms institutional buying at support
  • Enter at open of the very next candle after confirmation
  • Stop Loss = below the support zone / below candle low (whichever is lower)
  • Target = next resistance level above. Use the chart — do not use fixed pip targets.
04 — Put Setup (At Resistance)

BUY PUT — PRICE AT RESISTANCE

When price arrives at a strong resistance level, you are looking for a bearish reversal candle to confirm the rejection. Here are the 3 best candle signals at resistance:

Bearish Engulfing

Red body fully covers prior green body at resistance. The strongest rejection signal at a resistance zone. Combined with RSI above 50 turning down.

Shooting Star

Small body at bottom, long upper wick (2× body). Buyers tried to push higher but sellers rejected. Classic resistance rejection candle.

Bearish Pin Bar

Long upper wick that pierces above resistance but closes back below it. Price "tested" resistance and was strongly rejected — high-confidence put signal.

↓
PUT ENTRY — FULL CHECKLIST
  • Price is at a pre-marked resistance level (PDH, round number, VWAP, swing high)
  • Bearish reversal candle closes at or touching the resistance zone (Engulfing / Shooting Star / Pin Bar)
  • RSI is above 50 and turning down — or ideally in the 55–70 zone falling. Divergence adds maximum confidence.
  • Volume spike on the reversal candle — confirms institutional selling at resistance
  • Enter at open of the very next candle after confirmation
  • Stop Loss = above the resistance zone / above candle high (whichever is higher)
  • Target = next support level below. Let the chart define your target, not a fixed number.
05 — Advanced Concept

THE S/R FLIP — MOST POWERFUL LEVEL

An S/R Flip happens when a broken support becomes new resistance, or a broken resistance becomes new support. This is the single most powerful level in price action trading and is heavily used by professionals on Nifty.

↑
RESISTANCE → SUPPORT
  • Price breaks above a resistance level
  • Price pulls back to retest that former resistance
  • Bullish candle forms (Engulfing / Hammer) at the old resistance
  • RSI holds above 50 on the pullback
  • This is a BUY CALL with very high confidence
  • SL: Below the flipped support level
↓
SUPPORT → RESISTANCE
  • Price breaks below a support level
  • Price bounces back up to retest that former support
  • Bearish candle forms (Engulfing / Shooting Star) at old support
  • RSI stays below 50 on the bounce
  • This is a BUY PUT with very high confidence
  • SL: Above the flipped resistance level

🏆 Why the Flip Works: When price breaks a key level, all traders who were holding positions at that level are now trapped. When price comes back to retest, those trapped traders add to the move — creating a self-fulfilling prophecy. This is why S/R flips are the most reliable entries in intraday trading.

07 — Full Workflow

PRE-MARKET TO TRADE: STEP BY STEP

  1. 1
    Pre-Market (8:45–9:15 AM): Open your 15-min Nifty chart. Mark PDH, PDL, previous week high/low, round numbers nearest to current price, and previous day close. These are your levels for the day — do not change them during the session.
  2. 2
    Market Open (9:15–9:30 AM): Watch — do not trade. Let price discover its direction. Note whether Nifty opens above or below VWAP, and whether it immediately tests a key level. This tells you the day's likely bias.
  3. 3
    Identify Setup (Ongoing): Watch for price approaching one of your pre-marked S/R levels. As it approaches, go to 5-min chart for more detail. Look for slowing momentum (smaller candles, wicks appearing) as price nears the level.
  4. 4
    Wait for Candle Signal: Do not enter just because price is at S/R. Wait for a confirming reversal candle to fully close at the level — Bullish Engulfing / Hammer for Call. Bearish Engulfing / Shooting Star for Put.
  5. 5
    Check RSI: At the close of the signal candle, RSI must confirm direction. Below 50 rising = bullish. Above 50 falling = bearish. If RSI disagrees — skip the trade, no exceptions.
  6. 6
    Check Volume: Signal candle must show a volume spike. Zoom out — is this candle's volume noticeably bigger than the previous 3–4 candles? If yes, institutional players are active at this level. Green light.
  7. 7
    Enter + Set SL Immediately: Buy ATM Call or Put at the open of the next candle. The moment you enter, set your stop loss at the other side of the S/R zone. SL for Call = below support. SL for Put = above resistance.
  8. 8
    Target = Next S/R Level: Look at your pre-marked levels. For a Call from support, the target is the nearest resistance above. For a Put from resistance, the target is the nearest support below. This gives you a real, chart-based target.
  9. 9
    Trail & Exit: Once price moves 50% toward target, move SL to breakeven. Trail to each new candle's low (for calls) or high (for puts). Exit at target level or when an opposite reversal candle appears — whichever comes first.
